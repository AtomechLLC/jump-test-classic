It's all in the impact

A hit that feels good is a stack of small effects fired on a precise schedule. This page is rebuilt from the data inside Ruiz's actual demo build: nine effect layers, three audio layers, and a contact that cascades over five frames instead of landing on one.

Two authentic details worth stealing: the hit-freeze is baked into the animation clips as a "Hiccup" hold (the world keeps moving — only the actors stop), and the camera shake is smooth noise with a sine-eased decay and a hard max-offset clamp, not random jitter.

Press Z for the full attack. Press 1–9 to fire one ingredient by itself, exactly like the original demo. Untick layers below to cut them from the full attack.
